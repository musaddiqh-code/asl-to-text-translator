ℹ️ About

Making ASL
accessible to all

SignBridge is a browser-based tool for translating between American Sign Language gestures and written text — in real time. It was built to make communication more accessible for the Deaf and Hard of Hearing community.

American Sign Language is the primary language for over 500,000 people in the United States and Canada. It is a complete, natural language with its own grammar, syntax, and cultural richness — distinct from English.

This tool uses computer vision to track hand landmarks and a machine learning classifier to match them to ASL letters — running entirely in your browser with no data sent to any server.

🎯
Purpose
To reduce communication barriers between hearing and Deaf communities by making ASL translation fast, free, and accessible directly in the browser.
🔬
How It Works
MediaPipe Hands extracts 21 3D landmarks per frame. These are normalised (wrist = origin, unit bounding box) and fed to a Random Forest classifier trained on ASL letter samples. Predictions are smoothed over a 10-frame window.
🔒
Privacy
All processing happens on your device. No video frames, no landmark data, and no text is ever sent to a server. Your camera feed never leaves your browser.
